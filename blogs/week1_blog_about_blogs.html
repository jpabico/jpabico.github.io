Metablogging - A Blog About Blogs

March 14, 2014

I’m ambivalent about blogging. On one hand, I find it useful to write down one’s thoughts. On the other, I don’t think there’s any need to share your opinions with others if no one asks for them. I believe in freedom of speech and freedom of expression, but I hate it when people try to force their ideas upon me. That’s a bit much, I know.

I wouldn’t mind blogging so much if I didn’t already take notes. Writing down my ideas makes it easier for me to solidify my thoughts. But I’m already taking notes. I also keep a personal journal. I feel like I’m repeating myself more than I’d like. It gets boring reading my thoughts over and over again (I wonder if this is how others feel when they listen to me?). When time is short and I have a lot of things to do, I feel blogging is cumbersome. But when time is plentiful and I’ve completed my to-do list for the week, it’s nice to sit and unload my thoughts.

In short…I’m ambivalent about blogging.
