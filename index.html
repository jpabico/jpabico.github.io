Jury

"Always vote on principle, though you may vote alone, and you may cherish the sweetest reflection that your vote is never lost."

- John Adams

Jury is a web application that allows 2 parties to submit their arguments to the court of public opinion for resolution. Its social-media-based struture allows users to share thoughts and ideas. Educators can use this tool to help facilitate learning and discussion by involving students outside of the classroom.

Some key features of Jury allow users to:

  • Open new cases and make their arguments
  • Upload videos from YouTube to support their cases
  • Make a rebuttal to their opponents' cases
  • Comment on each party's argument and support their causes
  • Upvote and downvote comments
  • Vote on cases and decided the winner!

Jury is a Ruby on Rails web app making use of technologies such as ActiveRecord, HTML5, CSS3, Javascript, AJAX, and APIs (YouTube, CRON).

Letter Stream

"The more you read, the more things you will know. The more that you learn, the more places you'll go."

- Dr. Seuss

Letter Stream is an educational web app that helps children improve their reading skills. Found "at the end of The Reading Rainbow", children can develop their creativity and write their own stories, adjust the speed and color of the reading highlighter, and upload text files for more practice.

Designed with the Sinatra framework, Letter Stream uses HTML5, CSS3, Javascript, ActiveRecord, PostgreSQL and Ruby.

Try Letter Stream

Qu-est

"What we want to see is the child in pursuit of knowledge, and not the knowledge in pursuit of the child."

- George Bernard Shaw

Quest is an educational web app that allows students to assess one another inside and outside the classroom.

Students can write a Quest (QUiz-tEST question) and send it via Twitter to his/her fellow classmates. Every student has the opportunity to answer questions and get immediate feedback.

In addition, teachers can see the results of the entire class for every question, allowing for better planning and instructional design.
